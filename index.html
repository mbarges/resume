Bird-friendly Building Policy Researcher and Advocate
  • CV
My research investigates the dynamics driving the adoption (and non-adoption) of municipal bird-friendly building policies in the United States and Canada. Combining public policy analysis and environmental ethics, I seek to explain why some cities have mandatory bird-friendly building policies and others don't.

As an advocate, I organize and partner with community groups to push for better rules to protect birds from building collisions and light pollution. In 2023, I co-led a successful statewide campaign to pass Connecticut's lights out bill, CT Public Act 23-143, which requires all CT state agencies to turn off unnecessary outdoor nighttime lights at state-owned buildings year-round for birds. In 2024, I worked with local advocates to convince the Town of Greenwich to adopt a new lighting law, which is the most comprehensive lighting law in Connecticut. In 2025, I helped convinced the SUNY School of Environmental Science and Forestry to adopt a bird-friendly building policy -- and organized a community- and art-based mitigation plan for high-collision glass windscreens on our Syracuse campus. My goal is to help people and decision makers to appreciate the complex lives of birds and to understand how decisions about building, lighting, and landscaping impact birds and human communities.
