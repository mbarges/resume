Bird-friendly Building Policy Researcher and Advocate
  • CV
My dissertation project investigates the dynamics driving the adoption (and non-adoption) of mandatory bird-friendly building policies in U.S. and Canadian cities. I combine public policy analysis, environmental ethics, and urban political ecology to explain why some cities have mandatory bird-friendly building policies and others don't.

As a bird conservationist, I help people and decision makers to appreciate the complex lives of birds and how our decisions about building, lighting, and landscaping affect birds and human communities. I organize and partner with community groups to push for better rules to protect birds from building collisions and light pollution. In 2023, I co-led a successful statewide campaign to pass CT Public Act 23-143, Connecticut's lights out bill, which requires all CT state agencies to turn off unnecessary outdoor nighttime lights at state-owned buildings year-round for birds. In 2024, I worked with local advocates to convince the Town of Greenwich to adopt a Lighting Zoning Code Amendment comprising the most comprehensive municipal lighting law in Connecticut.

In 2025, I helped convinced the SUNY School of Environmental Science and Forestry to adopt a mandatory bird-friendly building policy for all new buildings and major retrofits as part of its 2025 Sustainability Action Plan. As a graduate student, I also led a community, art-based bird collision mitigation project community, art-based bird collision mitigation project that retrofitted several glass windscreens on campus.
